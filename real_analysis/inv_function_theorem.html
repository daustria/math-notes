The Inverse Function Theorem¶

Theorem

Let \(F : W \to \mathbb{R}^n\) be a \(C^r\) mapping, \(r = 1,2, \ldots\) or \(\infty\). Suppose \(a \in W\) with \(DF(a)\) a non-singular matrix. Then there is a neighbourhood \(U\) of W containing \(a\) such that \(V = F(U)\) is open and \(F|_U : U \to V\) is a diffeomorphism.

Moreover, we have the following formula for the differential of \(F^{-1}\)

\[DF^{-1}(y) = (DF(x))^{-1} \quad x \in U, y = F(x).\]
